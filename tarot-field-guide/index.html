What is Tarot

Tarot is a symbolic card system used for reflection, intuition, and personal insight. A traditional tarot deck contains 78 cards divided into two groups: the Major Arcana and the Minor Arcana. Each card represents themes, emotions, experiences, and archetypes that can offer guidance during a reading.The Major Arcana focuses on major life lessons and transformation, while the Minor Arcana reflects everyday experiences and challenges through the four suits: Cups, Pentacles, Swords, and Wands.

Although tarot is often associated with fortune telling, many people use it as a tool for introspection, mindfulness, and personal growth. Rather than predicting a fixed future, tarot encourages interpretation intuition, and deeper understanding of oneself and the situations surrounding them.

Major Arcana

The Major Arcana consists of 22 cards that represent major life themes, personal growth, and powerful experiences. These cards are often viewed as the core of the tarot deck because they focus on significant moments, lessons, and turning points that can shape a person’s journey.

Unlike the Minor Arcana, which reflects everyday situations and challenges, the Major Arcana explores broader emotional and spiritual themes. The cards are commonly interpreted as symbols of change, self-discovery, balance, and transformation, making them some of the most recognized and influential cards within tarot.

Minor Arcana

There are four different suits in a tarot deck: Cups, Pentacles, Swords, and Wands. Each suit represents a different area of life and helps organize the themes and meanings found within the Minor Arcana. While the Major Arcana focuses on larger life lessons and transformation, the four suits reflect everyday experiences, emotions, challenges, and relationships.

Each suit is connected to its own symbolic element and energy. Cups are associated with emotions and relationships, Pentacles focus on work and stability, Swords relate to thoughts and communication, and Wands represent creativity and ambition. Together, the four suits create a balanced system that explores many aspects of human experience and personal growth.

Reading Tarot

Reading tarot is the practice of interpreting cards to gain insight, perspective, and guidance. During a reading, cards are drawn and arranged in a spread, with each card carrying its own symbolism and meaning. Readers often consider the imagery, position of the cards, and their connection to one another to better understand the message of the reading.

There is no single “correct” way to read tarot, and many people develop their own style over time. Some readers focus on traditional meanings, while others rely more on intuition and personal interpretation. Tarot readings are commonly used for reflection, decision-making, journaling, and exploring emotions or life situations from a new perspective.

Basic Steps of a Tarot Reading

  1. Shuffle the deck and focus on a question or intention.
  2. Draw cards from the deck.
  3. Arrange the cards into a spread.
  4. Interpret the imagery and symbolism.
  5. Reflect on the overall message of the reading.

Common Spreads

Common tarot spreads are layouts used to organize cards during a reading. Each position within a spread has a specific meaning, helping provide structure and context for interpreting the cards. Some spreads are simple and beginner-friendly, while others are more detailed and explore situations in greater depth.

Different spreads are used for different purposes, such as gaining clarity, reflecting on emotions, exploring relationships, or understanding future possibilities. The arrangement of the cards can influence how the reading is interpreted, making spreads an important part of the tarot reading process.

Common Tarot Spreads

  • Single Card Spread: A simple draw used for quick insight or daily guidance.
  • Three-Card Spread: Often represents past, present, and future.
  • Celtic Cross Spread: A detailed spread used for deeper readings and complex situations.
  • Relationship Spread: Focuses on emotional dynamics and connections between people.
  • Decision-Making Spread: Helps compare options and possible outcomes.

Symbolism

Symbolism plays an important role in tarot and helps give each card its meaning and emotional tone. Tarot cards are filled with visual details such as colors, animals, celestial imagery, objects, and natural elements that can represent ideas, emotions, or experiences. These symbols help readers interpret the deeper messages within a reading.

Although symbolism can vary slightly between different tarot decks, many images appear repeatedly throughout tarot because they carry common meanings and themes. Learning to recognize these symbols can help readers better understand the cards, strengthen intuition, and notice patterns or connections during a reading.
