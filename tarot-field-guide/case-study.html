Design Decisions

Many of my design decisions were guided by both my personal aesthetic preferences and the overall mood associated with tarot. I chose a deep purple color palette because purple is one of my favorite colors, but it also worked well with the m ysterious and moody atmosphere I wanted the site to have. The glowing borders, dark b ackgrounds, and decorative imagery were all inspired by the symbolism and mystical qualities often connected to tarot cards and spiritual themes.

I also focused heavily on clean line aesthetics and overall visual balance throughout the layout. Even though the site has a darker visual style, I still wanted it to feel organized, readable, and intentional. I used my growing knowledge of typography and design principles to guide spacing, hierarchy, alignment, and font choices. I selected contrasting fonts to create a balance between elegance and readability while helping the headings stand out visually from the body content.

Technical Challenges

One of the biggest technical challenges I faced during this project was building the site structure correctly from the beginning. Initially, I was able to create a page that looked visually appealing, but the actual code foundation was not functioning the way it needed to. My layout was not properly responsive, and as the project progressed, I realized that some of my early structural decisions were making things more difficult later on. I think feeling overwhelmed and running short on time contributed to some of those problems because I started ocusing more on trying to make things work quickly rather than slowing down and thinking through the layout system carefully.

I also realized that my strengths and weaknesses in coding are very different. I feel fairly confident when it comes to targeting elements, adjusting styling, and making visual refinements, but creating the correct structural foundation with Flexbox and responsive layouts was much more difficult for me. Once the structure is set up correctly, I can usually work with it well, but figuring out how all the pieces should connect initially was the hardest part. Travis graciously helped guide me back in the right direction, and that honestly helped a lot. After restructuring parts of the layout properly, many of the responsive issues became much easier to solve.

Responsive Strategy

When adapting the site for mobile devices, my main goal was to simplify the layout while still maintaining the overall visual style and atmosphere of the design. One of the biggest changes I made was removing the decorative elements at smaller viewport sizes. Since many of those elements were absolutely positioned, they began to interfere with the layout and readability when the screen became too narrow. Hiding them on mobile helped the design scale down much more smoothly and made the content easier to read and navigate.

I also used media queries to restructure the layout for smaller screens. The sidebar navigation changed from a side layout into a stacked layout, content rows shifted into single columns, and footer buttons stacked vertically for better spacing and usability. I adjusted font sizes, image scaling, spacing, and content widths so the site would remain readable without horizontal scrolling. Throughout the process, I tried to preserve the mood and visual identity of the desktop design while making the experience more practical and accessible on smaller devices.

Learning Moments

One of the biggest learning moments for me during this project was understanding how important the initial layout structure is when working with Flexbox. Early on, I was mostly focused on making the design look visually correct, but I eventually realized that if the structural foundation is not built properly, responsiveness becomes much harder later. I learned that Flexbox works best when containers and parent-child relationships are planned intentionally from the start. Once I restructured my layout correctly using shared flex containers, many of the alignment and responsiveness issues became much easier to solve.

I also learned a lot about CSS positioning, especially the difference between normal layout flow and absolute positioning. Before this project, I did not fully understand how absolutely positioned elements interact with their parent containers. Through trial and error, I learned how position: relative and position: absolute work together and how decorative elements can be positioned independently from the rest of the content. I also gained a better understanding of media queries and responsive adjustments, especially how small CSS changes can completely change the behavior of a layout across different screen sizes.

Future Improvements

If I had more time to continue developing this project, I would like to experiment more with the placement and behavior of the absolutely positioned decorative images throughout the site. While I was able to make them work visually, I still feel like there is more I could explore in terms of layering, positioning, and integrating them more naturally into the layout. I think additional time would have allowed me to become more confident and intentional with those design choices instead of relying as much on trial and error.

I would also like to spend more time working directly with the code itself to continue strengthening my understanding of layout structure and responsiveness. Throughout this project, I realized that I learn best by actively building and troubleshooting, even when the process becomes frustrating. Although I still struggle with creating the correct structural foundation at times, I feel much more comfortable with CSS and responsive design concepts now than I did at the start of the project. With more practice and time, I think I could build future projects with much more confidence and independence.
